Central Processing Unit (CPU)

A central processing unit (CPU), also called a central processor or main processor, is the electronic circuitry that executes instructions comprising a computer program.

01001001 01001110 01010100 01000101 01001100

The CPU performs basic arithmetic, logic, controlling, and input/output (I/O) operations specified by the instructions in the program.

Fundamental components of a CPU include the arithmetic–logic unit (ALU) that performs arithmetic and logical operations.

Microarchitecture and Design

Modern CPUs are implemented on integrated circuit (IC) chips, with one or more CPUs on a single IC chip called a microprocessor.
